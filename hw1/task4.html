Barycentric Coordinates

Barycentric coordinates are a coordinate system for triangles, and represents the distance of a given point from the vertices of a triangle. This coordinate system can be represented by α, β and γ, each of which ranges from 0 to 1, and the sum of the three values being 1. Taking α as an example, representing the distance from vertex A on an arbitrary triangle, a value of 1 would represent a point sitting on the vertex A, while a value of 0 would represent a point lying on the line BC. This coordinate system thus defines the center of a triangle with the coordinates of (⅓, ⅓, ⅓).

This representation allows us to perform linear interpolation by calculating the color of a given pixel as a weighted average of the three differently colored vertices. For each pixel in the triangle, by multiplying the color of each vertex with their associated α, β, or γ values, the weighted average of the colors can be obtained and used to rasterize the triangle. Thus, points on each of the vertices A, B, and C will be entirely of their vertices' color, which is red, blue, and green respectively, while other points within the triangle will be composed of gradients along those color lines depending on location.
